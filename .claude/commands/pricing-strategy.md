## 2. Pricing Model Selection
| Model | Description | Best For | Risk |
|-------|-----------|---------|------|
| **Cost-plus** | COGS + target margin | Commodity, standard parts | Leaves money on table |
| **Value-based** | Price to customer value delivered | Differentiated, specialty | Requires deep customer insight |
| **Competitive parity** | Match market price | Commodity, second source | Race to bottom |
| **Penetration** | Below market to win share | New product launch, socket win | Margin pressure, hard to raise |
| **Skimming** | Premium for early adopters | First-to-market, innovation | Invites competition |
| **Tiered volume** | Decreasing ASP at volume | High-volume consumer/industrial | Forecast dependency |

## 8. ASP Erosion Management
| Factor | Impact | Mitigation |
|--------|--------|------------|
| Annual price-down (customer request) | -3-5%/yr | Offset with new product ASP, value add |
| Competitive pressure | -5-10% | Differentiate on performance, support, supply |
| Volume increase | -2-5% | Tiered pricing already accounts for this |
| Technology node advance | -10-15% | New product introduction at higher ASP |
| Currency fluctuation | Variable | Pricing in local currency, hedging |

## 9. New Product Pricing Process
1. **Market research**: Competitive pricing, customer willingness to pay
2. **Cost modeling**: COGS, yield, packaging, test costs
3. **Value quantification**: Customer savings, performance improvement
4. **Price setting**: Select model, set list + floor + target
5. **Channel alignment**: Disti margin, design registration, rebates
6. **Launch**: Communicate pricing to sales + channel
7. **Monitor**: Track win rate, competitive feedback, adjust quarterly
