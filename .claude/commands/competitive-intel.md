# Competitive Intelligence — Research and analyze competitors

Gather and analyze competitive intelligence on "$ARGUMENTS":

## 1. Competitor Profile
- Company overview: founded, HQ, size, funding, revenue (est.)
- Mission/positioning: how they describe themselves
- Target market: ICP, verticals, company size
- Pricing model: tiers, packaging, public pricing
- Go-to-market: direct sales, PLG, channel, hybrid

## 2. Product Analysis
| Capability | Them | Us | Advantage |
|-----------|------|----|-----------| 
| | | | |

- Core features and unique differentiators
- Technology stack and architecture
- Integration ecosystem (partners, APIs, marketplace)
- Known limitations or gaps (from reviews, forums, churned customers)

## 3. Market Perception
- G2/Capterra/TrustRadius ratings and review themes
- Analyst coverage (Gartner, Forrester positioning)
- Social media sentiment and thought leadership presence
- Customer testimonials and case studies they highlight
- Glassdoor employee sentiment (product/eng team morale)

## 5. Battle Card
### When We Win Against Them
- Scenarios where we have clear advantage
- Questions that expose their weaknesses
- Proof points and customer stories

### When We Lose Against Them
- Scenarios where they have advantage
- How to neutralize their strengths
- When to compete vs when to walk away

### Landmines to Set
Questions to ask the prospect that highlight competitor gaps:
1. "How do they handle [our strength area]?"
2. "Have you asked about [their known limitation]?"
3. "What happens when [scale/edge case they struggle with]?"

### Traps to Avoid
Topics they'll try to steer the conversation toward:
1. [Their strength area] — redirect to [our differentiator]
2. [Feature we lack] — reframe to business outcome

## 6. Strategic Recommendations
- **Positioning**: How to position against them in different scenarios
- **Messaging**: Key phrases that resonate when competing
- **Pricing**: How to handle pricing comparisons
- **Proof**: Which customer stories to deploy
- **Timing**: When in the cycle to introduce competitive differentiation

## 7. Monitoring Plan
- Track: product launches, pricing changes, leadership moves, funding
- Sources: press releases, job postings, patent filings, conference talks
- Frequency: Monthly refresh, real-time for major events
