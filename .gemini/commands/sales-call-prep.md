# Sales Call Prep — Prepare for a sales meeting

Prepare a comprehensive call brief for meeting with "$ARGUMENTS":

## 1. Pre-Call Research
- Refresh account intelligence (company, contacts, recent activity)
- Review all prior interactions (emails, calls, notes in CRM)
- Check LinkedIn for recent posts/activity from the attendees
- Identify any trigger events since last contact

## 2. Meeting Agenda (propose)
| Time | Topic | Owner |
|------|-------|-------|
| 0-5 min | Rapport + confirm agenda | You |
| 5-15 min | Discovery: their situation, pain, impact | Them (you ask questions) |
| 15-25 min | Solution alignment: map pain to capabilities | You |
| 25-28 min | Next steps: mutual action plan | Both |
| 28-30 min | Confirm actions + timeline | You |

## 3. Discovery Questions (prepare 10)
Tiered by depth:
- **Situational** (2): Current state, tools, process
- **Problem** (3): What's not working, impact on team/revenue
- **Implication** (3): What happens if unsolved, downstream effects
- **Need-Payoff** (2): What would ideal look like, ROI expectations

## 4. Objection Handling Matrix
| Likely Objection | Response Framework |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| "Too expensive" | Reframe to cost of inaction + ROI |
| "We have a solution" | Acknowledge, probe gaps, differentiate |
| "Not the right time" | Understand timeline, offer low-commitment next step |
| "Need to involve others" | Offer to help build internal business case |
| "Send me info" | Agree, but secure a follow-up date first |

## 5. Success Criteria
Define what "good" looks like for this call:
- [ ] Confirmed pain point with quantified impact
- [ ] Identified decision process and timeline
- [ ] Agreed on specific next step with date
- [ ] Got commitment to involve economic buyer (if not present)

## 6. Post-Call Template
After the meeting, capture:
- Key takeaways (3 bullets)
- Action items (who does what by when)
- Deal stage update recommendation
- Follow-up email draft (send within 2 hours)
