## 6. Patterns & Anti-Patterns Discovered

### Patterns (leverage these)
1. **Indian IT services whitespace**: No Infosys/TCS/Wipro/HCL has ever sponsored GSA EEF in 20 years → "First Indian-heritage semiconductor services sponsor" positioning
2. **imec dual presence**: imec-int.com (research) AND imeciclink.com (commercial) both attend → two engagement tracks for partnership
3. **VIP dinner = highest value**: BMW World-caliber event → relationship quality > quantity
4. **Day 1 imec workshop = intimate access**: 8:30am workshop is more intimate than main forum → prioritize Day 1 for imec engagement
5. **New CEO receptivity**: Patrick Vandenameele's debut as CEO → open to new partnerships, fresh mandate

### Anti-Patterns (avoid these)
1. **Generic Infosys pitch**: "We offer engineering services" → MUST be semiconductor-specific, driver-specific
2. **Attending without sponsoring**: Lost in crowd of 300, no brand visibility → minimum Gold sponsorship
3. **Single-threading IMEC**: Only talking to Bernard → engage Patrick Vandenameele, IC-link team, workshop speakers
4. **No pre-scheduled meetings**: Showing up and hoping for hallway conversations → pre-book 15-20 meetings via GSA platform + LinkedIn
5. **Missing follow-up**: Collecting business cards without same-day follow-up → CRM entry + thank-you email within 24 hours
